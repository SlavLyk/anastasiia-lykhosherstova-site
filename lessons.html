Levels Taught

Who I
Teach

From a child's very first bow hold to advanced repertoire and competition preparation — all ages and levels are welcome. Every student begins with a complimentary assessment lesson.

01
Beginner
Ages 5+ · Adults Welcome

No experience necessary. We start with posture, bow hold, open strings, and simple melodies — building confidence and a lifelong love of music.

  • Instrument sizing & setup guidance
  • Proper posture & bow technique
  • Note reading & basic music theory
  • First repertoire pieces
  • Relaxed, encouraging pace
02
Intermediate
Grades 3–7 · RCM Aligned

Building on existing skills with a focus on tone production, shifting, vibrato, and expanding repertoire into classical and contemporary works.

  • Vibrato development & shifting
  • Scales, arpeggios & études
  • Classical & modern repertoire
  • RCM examination preparation
  • Chamber & ensemble skills
03
Advanced
Grades 8+ · Pre-Conservatoire

Serious students preparing for conservatoire auditions, youth orchestra trials, or competitions. Demanding repertoire with concert-level attention to detail.

  • Concerto & sonata repertoire
  • Advanced bowing techniques
  • Performance psychology & stage craft
  • Audition & competition coaching
  • Music theory at post-secondary level
What You'll Learn

Four Pillars of
Every Lesson

01
Technique

Bowing mechanics, left-hand precision, intonation, shifting, vibrato, and tone production — the physical foundations that make everything else possible.

02
Music Theory

Reading notation, understanding keys and harmony, rhythm literacy, and the theoretical knowledge that turns a player into a musician.

03
Repertoire

Works chosen to challenge and delight — from Suzuki and Bach to Romantic concertos and contemporary Canadian composers.

04
Performance

Stage presence, managing nerves, communicating musically, and the confidence to share your playing — because music is meant to be heard.
